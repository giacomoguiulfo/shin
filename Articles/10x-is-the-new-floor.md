# 10x Is the New Floor

## Key Ideas

AI hasn't made the best engineers better — it raised the floor so the "10x engineer" output is now baseline for anyone who's truly adopted the tools. The mythical 10x didn't level up; everyone else caught up.

**Two realities coexist:** Founders rewiring workflows weekly and maxing token limits before lunch vs. Fortune 500 execs whose mental models froze at "LLMs can't count Rs in strawberry." Same dinner table, different centuries.

**The conductor model:** Top performers hold all the context — the feeling, the texture — in their head while orchestrating a fleet of agents for execution. The job is hearing when something's off across a dozen parallel tracks.

**The reliable-competence archetype is endangered:** The VP-of-Product profile — 16 years of big tech, hits deadlines, $300K trajectory — is exactly what AI tools have caught up to. Built a career on process, not agency.

**Talent bell curve is splitting:** The old distribution (cluster around average, get promoted on schedule) is bifurcating. AI is "amplification intelligence" — it reveals who already had agency and curiosity, and amplifies the gap.

**Block as proof point:** 40% headcount cut, stock up 24%. Jack Dorsey's thesis: smaller teams + AI tools = better output.

## Reaction

The "floor rose to meet them" framing is sharper than most takes. It's not about AI making superstars — it's about compressing the distribution. The 10x engineer was always just someone with agency + deep context + fast iteration. AI gives everyone the fast iteration part. Agency and context remain human.

The conductor metaphor echoes [[developer-to-fleet-commander|Alfred Lin's fleet commander]] almost exactly — both posted the same day. Something is crystallizing in the discourse.

Where I push back: the "reliable competence is dead" angle is too clean. Someone still needs to keep the trains running while the conductors are orchestrating agents. The failure mode of an all-conductors org is chaos with no one minding the rails. The real shift is that reliable competence alone is no longer *sufficient* — you need it AND agency.
