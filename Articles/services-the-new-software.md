# Services: The New Software

## Key Ideas

AI enables selling **finished work** (autopilots) instead of **tools** (copilots). The next $1T company will be a software company masquerading as a services firm.

**Framework — two axes:**
- **Intelligence** (rule-following) vs. **Judgment** (experience-based decisions)
- **Copilots** (sell tools, tool budget) vs. **Autopilots** (sell work, work budget)

Work budgets >> tool budgets → autopilots capture more value.

**The playbook:** Start as autopilot in outsourced, intelligence-heavy work (insurance, accounting, medical coding), then expand into judgment as domain data compounds.

**The convergence:** As autopilots accumulate proprietary data, judgment work becomes automatable. Copilots and autopilots merge — starting position determines who wins.

**Innovator's dilemma:** Copilots going autopilot means cutting out their own customers. Most won't, so autopilots win by default.

## Our Take

- The intelligence vs. judgment framework is clean and useful.
- **Counter-argument on copilots:** Copilots sit next to experts and see richer judgment signals — they have the *data* advantage for learning judgment. The real blocker isn't capability, it's **distribution** — autopilots sell to the check-writer, copilots have to completely re-do their GTM.
- Likely outcome: copilots that are willing to blow up their GTM win, but most won't, so autopilots win by default.
- The innovator's dilemma framing is convenient but not inevitable — Adobe, Intuit show companies *can* cannibalize when margins justify it.
