# Jevons Paradox Doesn't Always Hold — When Automation Genuinely Obsoletes

## The Takeaway

Not all automation creates more demand and more jobs. The "Jevons Paradox" applies to *process efficiency* in demand-constrained markets. But when automation improves at both *creation and maintenance* of a deliverable, it can genuinely eliminate the role — because there's no backlog of work to absorb displaced labor.

## The Distinction

**Undershoot scenario (roles transform):** AI handles new code generation but still needs humans to supervise, architect, and maintain. Demand stays roughly constant or shifts to higher-leverage work.

**Overshoot scenario (roles disappear):** AI improves at both writing new code AND maintaining/debugging existing code at human or better levels. The entire pipeline — creation to maintenance — runs without human engineers. No backlog absorbs the displaced workers.

## The Signal to Watch

The key differentiator is **whether AI improves at maintenance and debt management, not just greenfield development.** Maintenance is traditionally thought of as easier than building from scratch, but if AI does both well and improving, that's the sign that even senior/supervision roles will face pressure.

## Rule of Thumb

When evaluating whether a technology will displace a profession:
- Does it only automate the easy part (writing new code)? → roles transform
- Does it automate both creation and maintenance? → roles may genuinely disappear
- Has it already improved 3x in 3 years? → extrapolate conservatively — the trend may accelerate
