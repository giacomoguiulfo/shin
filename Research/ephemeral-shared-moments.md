# Ephemeral Shared Moments

Research into time-bound, synchronized social experiences where the value comes from everyone participating at the same moment.

---

## Landscape

### The Big Players (Ephemeral/Synchronized Sharing)

**BeReal** (2020, France)
- Founded by Alexis Barreyat and Kevin Perreau. Core mechanic: one random daily notification, 2 minutes to shoot with front + back cameras simultaneously.
- Peaked at 73.5M MAU in August 2022. Dropped to ~23M by early 2024. Downloads fell 60% YoY in 2024 (12.7M), then another 50% in 2025.
- Acquired by mobile gaming company Voodoo in June 2024 for ~500M EUR. Now running ads (Nike, Netflix, Amazon among 200+ advertisers). Claims 40M MAU but third-party estimates put it closer to 16M.
- 85% Gen Z user base. 72% daily engagement among remaining active users. 68% open within 3 minutes of the notification.
- RealMojis (selfie-emoji reactions) replaced traditional likes. Friends-only by default.

**Snapchat** (2011)
- Pioneered ephemeral messaging and Stories. Now ~500M DAU. Stories get 65% completion rate, 3x more engagement than individual Snaps.
- Evolved far beyond ephemeral into AR, Snap Map (location sharing), Spotlight (TikTok-style feed), and AI features.
- Snap Map inherited Zenly's DNA after acquiring then shutting it down. AR geofilters at events are a key revenue driver.
- Meta reportedly building "Instants" (codename) as a direct Snapchat competitor, possible beta March 2026.

**Instagram Stories** (2016)
- Copied Snapchat Stories wholesale. Now the dominant ephemeral format globally. Close Friends list enables intimate sharing.
- Instagram Notes (60-character status bubbles, 2022) added lightweight ephemerality. Content Notes feature was later removed due to low adoption.
- Close Friends is expanding into more surfaces: notes, live features, feed experiments in some regions.

**Locket** (2022)
- Widget that lets you push photos directly to friends' home screens. Founded by Matt Moss as a gift for his girlfriend.
- 30M+ signups, ~800K monthly downloads, ~$300K monthly revenue. Won Apple's Cultural Impact Award.
- Raised $12.5M (investors include Sam Altman, Instagram co-founder Mike Krieger, Quora CEO Adam D'Angelo).
- Nearly 2 billion photos sent. No clear monetization model yet. The intimacy is the product.

**TenTen** (2024, France)
- Walkie-talkie app for close friends. Voice messages play through the recipient's phone without them answering or even unlocking it.
- 6M downloads, exploded via TikTok virality in late May 2024. Encrypted and ephemeral (messages not stored on servers).
- Controversial because it bypasses lock screens, causing disruptions in classrooms.
- Founded by Jule Colmar and Antoine Bache. Vision: "communicate like roommates."

### The Dead and Dying

**Poparazzi** (2021, shut down 2023)
- You could only post photos of friends, not yourself. Hit #1 on App Store at launch. Raised $15M Series A.
- Collapsed from 4M MAU to 2-3K MAU. Shut down May 2023, gave users until June 30 to download data.
- Died because the mechanic was a gimmick, not a habit. Once the novelty wore off, there was no reason to return.

**Dispo** (2019)
- Disposable camera simulation: take a photo, wait 24 hours to see it. Co-founded by David Dobrik.
- Raised $20M at $200M valuation (Spark Capital led). Dobrik forced out after Vlog Squad sexual assault allegations; Spark Capital severed all ties.
- Continued under CEO Daniel Liss with new investors (Kevin Durant, Annie Leibovitz, Seven Seven Six). Still operating but largely irrelevant. Frequent crash reports.

**Zenly** (2011, shut down Feb 2023)
- Real-time location sharing with friends on a beautiful map. Acquired by Snap for $200M+ in 2017.
- Had 40M users when Snap shut it down. Snap couldn't monetize it and didn't want to maintain two separate products.
- Beloved by users. The shutdown was controversial. Some ex-Zenly team members are reportedly building spiritual successors.

**Clubhouse** (2020)
- Live audio rooms. Peaked at 100M users, valued at $4B. Lost 80% of users within 6 months of peak.
- Failed because: pandemic-dependent, loss of exclusivity killed mystique, content quality collapsed at scale ("people suck at live"), no monetization, Twitter Spaces commoditized the format, laid off 50%+ of staff in 2023.

### Adjacent / Emerging

**Noplace** (2024)
- Text-based social network, Myspace-meets-Twitter for Gen Z. Real-time status updates about what you're currently doing. No photos or video.
- Hit #1 on App Store July 2024. Raised ~$19M total ($15M Series A1, $75M pre-money valuation). Investors: 776, Forerunner.

**Retro** (2023)
- Weekly photo journal for close friends. Founded by ex-Instagram engineers (Nathan Sharp led Instagram Stories launch in 2016).
- ~1M users. Recently added "time travel" through camera roll memories. The anti-viral social app.

**Collective Memory** (2025)
- Decentralized knowledge system for capturing real-world events and cultural moments. 1M+ "Memories" from 10K+ early adopters across nearly every country.
- Uses crypto-native prediction market mechanics (BELIEF token). Positioned at the intersection of AI, crypto, and collective memory.

## What's Broken

### The BeReal Trap: Synchronized Timing Without Depth

BeReal proved that synchronized timing creates an initial thrill but doesn't sustain engagement. The core problem:

1. **One-dimensional content.** A single photo of your desk, lunch, or commute is not interesting enough to check every day. As one Vice writer put it: BeReal "makes everyone look extremely boring." Instagram shows an aspirational lie; BeReal shows a mundane truth. Neither is what people actually want.

2. **No feed to browse.** Without a scrollable feed, there's nothing to do on the app besides post and react. Users open, post, see 8 friends' photos, close. Total session: 90 seconds. Compare to TikTok's infinite scroll. BeReal has no "one more" mechanic.

3. **Notification fatigue.** The random daily notification was novel for 2 months, then became annoying. Users started ignoring it, then stopped posting, then their friends stopped posting (network collapse).

4. **No accumulation of value.** Posts disappear. There's no profile to build, no history to revisit, no progression. Every day resets to zero. Users feel like they're shouting into a void.

### The Broader Ephemeral Paradox

Ephemeral formats have a fundamental tension: **the thing that makes them feel safe (disappearing) is the same thing that makes them feel pointless (nothing persists).**

- Snapchat solved this by making ephemeral messaging the gateway drug to a persistent platform (Stories, Discover, Snap Map, Spotlight). Ephemerality is one layer, not the whole product.
- Instagram Stories work because they sit atop a permanent profile. The ephemeral layer creates urgency; the permanent layer creates identity.
- Pure-play ephemeral apps (BeReal, Poparazzi, Dispo) have no persistent layer. They're all urgency, no identity.

### The Clubhouse Lesson: Synchronous Is Expensive

Live/synchronous experiences demand that users be available at the same time. This creates:

- **Scheduling friction.** The more synchronous the experience, the harder it is to fit into daily life.
- **Quality variance.** In async formats, bad content gets filtered out. In live formats, you get whatever shows up.
- **Pandemic dependency.** Clubhouse, BeReal, and Houseparty all peaked when people were stuck at home with nothing to do. When life resumed, synchronous social was the first thing cut.

### The Retention Death Spiral

Social apps have a specific failure mode:

1. Novelty drives initial downloads (press, virality, FOMO).
2. Core mechanic is too thin to sustain daily use.
3. Power users post less frequently. Their friends see fewer posts.
4. Friends stop checking. Network effects reverse.
5. The app becomes a ghost town. Downloads crater.

This played out identically at BeReal, Poparazzi, Clubhouse, and Dispo. Retention benchmarks for social apps are brutal: you need 25-30% D30 retention to have a viable business, and most ephemeral apps fall to single digits.

### The Monetization Gap

Ephemeral content is structurally hostile to advertising:

- Content disappears, so impressions are time-limited.
- Small, intimate friend groups mean low reach per post.
- "Authentic" positioning clashes with brand messaging (BeReal's ad rollout felt like betrayal to users).
- No persistent profiles means no targeting data accumulates.

## Business Opportunities

### 1. Event-Anchored Ephemeral (Location + Time)

The biggest whitespace: ephemeral sharing tied to real-world events rather than arbitrary daily notifications.

- **Concert/festival mode.** A shared ephemeral feed that only exists during the show, geofenced to the venue. Everyone at the concert contributes to a collective visual memory. The feed lives for 48 hours, then becomes a permanent "memory artifact" (collage, highlight reel, time-lapse).
- **Sports watch parties.** Synchronized reactions during live games. Think a BeReal-style notification at the moment of a goal/touchdown, capturing everyone's reaction simultaneously. The value is seeing your friends' faces at the exact same moment.
- **Travel moments.** Geofenced prompts at landmarks, natural wonders, or cultural sites. "You're at the Grand Canyon. Everyone here right now is sharing their view." Creates a collective memory from strangers sharing the same physical moment.

Revenue model: partnerships with venues, festivals, sports leagues. Premium "memory artifacts" (printed books, AR-enhanced replays). B2B licensing to event organizers.

### 2. Milestone/Achievement Moments

Instead of random timing, trigger shared moments around meaningful life events:

- **Fitness achievements.** You just ran your first 5K? Your friends get notified and have 5 minutes to send a video reaction. Integrates with Apple Watch, Strava, etc.
- **Learning milestones.** Finished a course, shipped a project, got a promotion. The moment is real and earned, not random.
- **Collective challenges.** A group of friends commits to a 30-day challenge. Daily check-ins are ephemeral, but completion unlocks a permanent shared artifact.

Revenue model: premium challenge templates, partnerships with fitness/education platforms, enterprise team versions.

### 3. Enterprise/Team Culture

The enterprise social networking market is projected to hit $34.4B by 2030. Remote teams (70% of global workforce by 2025 estimates) desperately need spontaneous human connection.

- **Team pulse moments.** Random daily prompt to a team: "Show us your workspace right now" or "What are you working on this second?" Lower-stakes than Slack, more human than standup meetings.
- **Onboarding rituals.** New hire's first week captured in ephemeral moments, creating a shared memory with the team.
- **Celebration moments.** Ship a feature? Close a deal? Trigger a 5-minute team reaction window. More spontaneous than a Slack emoji, more personal than a company-wide email.

Revenue model: per-seat SaaS pricing. Integrates with Slack, Teams, Notion. Sells to People/Culture teams.

### 4. AI-Curated Moment Detection

Use on-device AI to detect "moment-worthy" situations and trigger shared experiences:

- **Ambient awareness.** Phone detects you're at a sunset, a concert, a party (via audio, location, accelerometer, time of day). Prompts: "This looks like a moment. Share it with your people?"
- **Emotional resonance.** AI analyzes the photo/video you just took and scores its emotional intensity. High-intensity moments get surfaced to close friends with higher urgency.
- **Pattern matching.** "You and 3 friends are all at restaurants right now. Want to start a dinner moment?" Detects coincidental synchronicity and turns it into shared experience.

Revenue model: premium AI features, device-level partnerships (pre-installed on phones), data insights (anonymized) for venue/brand partners.

### 5. Scarcity-Driven Social Mechanics

Borrow from gaming and luxury goods: make sharing itself scarce and therefore valuable.

- **Limited daily "moment tokens."** You get 3 per week. Each one triggers a synchronized capture with your chosen group. Because they're scarce, each one feels meaningful. Friends know: "They used a token on me."
- **Seasonal/rare moments.** Certain triggers only happen rarely (solstice, eclipse, your city hitting a specific temperature). Scarcity creates anticipation and ritual.
- **Streak mechanics with stakes.** Unlike Snapchat streaks (which are trivial), tie streaks to meaningful shared activities. A 30-day streak of morning check-ins with your best friend unlocks a shared year-in-review.

Revenue model: sell additional moment tokens (carefully, to avoid pay-to-win feel). Premium moment types. Brand-sponsored rare moments.

## Breakthrough Ideas

### 1. "Time Capsule Moments" -- Ephemeral Now, Permanent Later

The fundamental fix for the ephemeral paradox: content is ephemeral in real-time but crystallizes into permanent artifacts after a threshold.

- During the moment: live, raw, synchronized. Everyone sees each other's unfiltered reactions in real-time.
- After 24 hours: the moment "develops" (like Dispo's delayed reveal, but applied to the group). AI assembles individual contributions into a single shared artifact -- a collage, a short film, a photo essay.
- After 1 year: the moment resurfaces as a memory. The group relives it together. New reactions are layered on top.

This solves both problems: the ephemerality creates urgency and authenticity in the moment, while the crystallization creates lasting value that accumulates over time. Your shared history becomes richer, not emptier.

### 2. "Witness" -- Collective Presence at Scale

A platform where being somewhere at the same time as strangers creates shared value.

- You're at a protest, a sunrise hike, a historic event, a concert. The app detects you're in a cluster of other users.
- Everyone's perspective is woven into a single multi-angle, multi-sensory record of the moment.
- After the event, participants can explore the collective memory: see what it looked like from every angle, hear what it sounded like from different positions, read what people were thinking.
- Participants become co-authors of a shared document of reality. Not social media -- social memory.

The business: license the technology to news organizations, documentarians, event producers. Premium access to historic collective memories. A living archive of human experience.

### 3. "Resonance" -- AI-Matched Moment Sharing Between Strangers

What if the synchronized moment wasn't with your existing friends, but with someone you've never met who's experiencing the exact same thing?

- You're watching a sunset alone in Portland. Someone in Lisbon is watching the same sunset (time zone offset). AI matches you.
- You both capture the moment simultaneously. You see each other's sunset side by side.
- No profiles, no follows, no history. Just two humans sharing one moment. The connection is the moment itself.
- Some resonances lead to lasting connections. Most are beautiful one-offs.

This is the opposite of algorithmic social media: instead of matching you with content, it matches you with consciousness.

### 4. "Campfire" -- Ritual-Based Micro-Communities

Small groups (3-8 people) who commit to a shared ritual: every Sunday at sunset, every Friday at lunch, the first snowfall of winter.

- The ritual defines the group, not the other way around. You don't add friends and then decide what to do. You choose a ritual and find (or invite) people who share it.
- Each ritual has a cadence and a trigger. Some are time-based (weekly). Some are event-based (first rain, full moon, power outage). Some are achievement-based (every time someone in the group PRs a lift).
- Over months, the ritual accumulates into a rich shared history. The campfire grows.
- Monetization: premium ritual templates, physical artifacts (printed ritual journals), partnerships with brands who want to create rituals around their products (a coffee brand sponsors "morning brew" rituals).

### 5. "Flash Mobs for the Feed" -- Coordinated Creative Moments

What if synchronized moments weren't just reactive (capture what's happening) but proactive (create something together)?

- The app issues a creative prompt at a random time: "Everyone point your camera at the sky right now" or "Show us something red within arm's reach" or "Make the same face as the person next to you."
- Individual responses are stitched into a collective artwork -- a mosaic of skies from 10,000 cities, a grid of red objects, a wall of mirrored expressions.
- The best collective artworks become NFTs, prints, or exhibitions. Contributors share in the value.
- This turns ephemeral moments from consumption into creation. You're not just witnessing -- you're co-creating.

### 6. The "Anti-Notification" -- Ambient Awareness Without Interruption

The notification model is broken. BeReal's notification became annoying. Clubhouse's notifications became spam. The next generation needs to work differently:

- Instead of push notifications, use ambient signals: a gentle glow on your lock screen widget when friends are "in a moment." No buzzing, no sound, no urgency.
- You drift in when you're ready, not when the app demands it. The moment has a window (30 minutes, not 2 minutes), and the window is visible but not aggressive.
- The result: moments feel discovered, not demanded. Like noticing the sunset rather than being told to look at it.
